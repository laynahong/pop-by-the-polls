Pop by the Polls

This is a short research article for the 2020 primaries. It covers four issues currently being discussed, a candidate running for President of the United States, a polling place, and a sample ballot.

Read More

Issues

These are the issues that voters and candidates are discussing this primary season.

Gun Reform

Mass shootings in the United States have caused Democrats to push for stricter gun regulations and reforms. On the other hand, Republicans invoke the Second Amendment right to bear arms, also pointing out that guns might be needed for self-defense.

Abortion

The Trump Administration and Republicans continue to try to criminalize abortions on social and religious basis. On the other hand, Democrats are pushing a more pro-choice agenda, reiterating that abortion is a personal choice.

Immigration

Immigration has also been a hot topic within national politics. While both parties agree on curbing illegal migration into the country, Democrats support making it easier for immigrants to become legal citizens, while Republicans want to restrict immigration all together.

Healthcare

Affordable healthcare is an important issue for Democrats, who want to expand the Affordable Health Care Act passed under the Obama Administration. Opponents of expanding Medicare argue against higher taxes.

Candidate

Joe Biden is running for President of the United States with Kamala Harris as his running mate.

Joe Biden

Joe Biden is the Democratic presidential nomination for the 2020 Election. His running mate is U.S Senator Kamala Harris. He served as Delaware State Senator from 1973-2009. He was Vice President of the United States from 2009-2016 under the Obama Administration. His platform for the 2020 Election is described by "Build Back Better."

Polling Place

This is a location that voters can visit to cast a ballot for the candidates they prefer.

Windsor Park Elementary School

If I were at home in Charlotte, my voting location would be Windsor Park Elementary School. This is where my mother and father will vote. This voting location is located at 3910 Sudbury Road Charlotte, NC 28205 For Election Day, it will open from 6:30 AM to 7:30 PM.
